Human Computer Interaction - Lecture 1

In the past,usability was not a concern, people interacted with the machines in their own language, in binary. People did not think that computers could be used for anything else a apart from work or calculations.

  • Due to this "language barrier" not everyone could use computers due to their difference/lack of education in this area.
  • Human computer interaction,HCI, is constantly changing and evolving, making computers accessible to everyone, regardless of their background or status.

Changes in HCI - Lecture 2

Over the years, software become more complex, it became context-aware.

  • Its like a gps system, it is aware of where you are and when you're on the move.
  • Some software has been adapted for visually impaired/blind people - screenreaders.
    • However, these screenreaders are not the best solution ever, they have a high execution time and are very prone to making errors.
  • As well as that, users' attitudes after using this software was not a positive one. This has been a challenge for many years.

Interfaces - Lecture 3

The question is, what makes a good interface?

  • A good interface must be easy to use, learn and remember, low execution time and user friendly
    • But how do you identify if the interface is user friendly?

      • There are many factors associated with users, however, the main ones are:

    • Age
    • Education
    • Computer Literacy
    Some applications need a considerable amount of specific knowledge - this creates 'gulfs' It must also aim to decrease/eliminate gulfs,which are gaps between the developers and the end-users.

    Human Memory - Lecture 4

    Humans receive data in different forms, via senses such as sight, sound and touch.

    • Humans store this data in two types of memory - short-term and long-term memory.
    • In STM, data is stored in very short periods of time, however, these periods of time may be increased in the absense of other stimuli/distractions.
        • Info passes from the STM into the LTM, due to repitition and the meaning of the info. If the short term memory is i any way damaged, then the data will be stored for an even sorter amount of time, and no new long term memories would be created. Basically, you could'nt learn any new info or skills.
